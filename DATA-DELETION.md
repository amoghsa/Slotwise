# Slotwise — Data Deletion

**Effective date:** 2026-05-30
**Last updated:** 2026-06-07

This page describes how to delete data associated with your use of Slotwise (iOS
and Android). It is published alongside the [Privacy Policy](./PRIVACY.md) to make
deletion easy to find and to satisfy Google Play's user-data-deletion requirement
(https://support.google.com/googleplay/android-developer/answer/13327111).

If you have any trouble, email **slotwise@amoghsa.com** or open an issue in
this repository — we'll respond within 30 days.

## Does Slotwise have accounts?

**No.** Slotwise has no sign-up, no login, and no server-side profile. There is
no account to delete — only on-device data.

## Is any of my data stored off my device?

**We hold no account or profile about you** — there is no server-side copy of your
household, kids, rules, or plans to erase. The app does send **anonymous usage
analytics** (Google Firebase Analytics — screens, taps, timings; no names or
plans), which you can turn off any time in **Settings → Privacy → Share anonymous
usage data**; these events carry no identity to delete. Deleting your on-device
data is entirely under your control. Two clarifications (see the
[Privacy Policy](./PRIVACY.md) for detail):

- If you have **device backup** enabled (iCloud on iOS, Google backup on Android),
  a copy of the app's on-device data may live in *your own* backup, which you
  manage in your device settings — we never receive it.
- Optional features (Discover, place search) send **anonymous request parameters**
  to fetch public listings; these are not tied to an account or stored as a profile
  about you.

## How to delete your data

You have two options, both immediate and complete:

- **Reset inside the app — Settings → Reset app.** Permanently wipes your
  household, kids, seasons, rules, activities, time slots, and chosen plans from
  the device. The app returns to its first-run state.
- **Uninstall the app.** Removes the app's entire local database along with the
  app.

## What about calendar files I exported?

If you used **Export** to create an `.ics` calendar file and imported it into
Apple Calendar, Google Calendar, or another app, that copy now lives in *that*
app. Slotwise cannot reach it. To remove those events, delete them in the
calendar app you imported them into.
